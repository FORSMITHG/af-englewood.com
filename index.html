Who We Are

Angstrom Fiber Group at Englewood, Ohio.

Angstrom Fiber Englewood is a division of Angstrom Fiber Group under Angstrom Automotive Group — a NMSDC-certified minority-owned Tier 1 supplier founded in 1999, with 15 companies across North America, South America, and Europe.

Our 105,000+ sq. ft. Englewood facility opened in 2018 as Hematite's U.S. headquarters, became Woodbridge Englewood (NVH Div.) in 2021, and joined the Angstrom Fiber Group family in 2023. We manufacture acoustics, airflow, water management, and structural fiber components for Ford, Stellantis, GM, Toyota, and other major OEMs.

Core Capabilities

Built for every corner of the vehicle.

Three core manufacturing processes — compression molding, vacuum forming, and fiber processing — backed by robotic waterjet, assembly, and full PPAP capability.

Compression Molding

Heat and pressure forming of fiber-reinforced composites, SMC, and GMT materials to net-shape automotive parts.

Vacuum Forming

Thermoforming and vacuum forming of plastic and composite sheet materials into complex automotive geometries.

Fiber Processing

Polyester and recycled fiber production, layup, and forming for NVH, underbody, and interior applications.

Robotic Waterjet Cutting

5-axis robotic waterjet trimming for precision edge profiles and complex three-dimensional geometries.

Assembly & Sub-Assembly

Lamination, hardware attachment, kitting, and OEM-compliant packaging for just-in-time delivery.

Engineering & PPAP

On-site engineering for DFMEA, prototype development, PPAP documentation, and customer-specific validation.

Sustainability

Recycled materials.
Production-grade results.

Our manufacturing heritage dates to 1978, when Hematite founder Charles Pavanel pioneered a method for converting PVC trim scrap — 16–20 million pounds per year filling landfills from just two Ford facilities — into high-quality reinstallable components. That green-first foundation is built into everything we make.

Today, Angstrom Group extends this across all locations through LED lighting conversion, supply chain footprint optimization, and active investment in EV-compatible NVH and thermal management components.
